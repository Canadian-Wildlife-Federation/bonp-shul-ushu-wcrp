Acknowledgements

This plan is the culmination of a collaborative planning process undertaken in the Eagle River, Bessette Creek and Bonaparte River watersheds over many months with a team of individuals and groups that are passionate about the conservation and restoration of freshwater ecosystems and the species they support. Plan development was funded by the BC Salmon Restoration and Innovation Fund and the Canada Nature Fund for Aquatic Species at Risk. We were fortunate to benefit from the feedback, guidance, and wisdom of many people who volunteered their time throughout this process — this publication would not have been possible without the engagement of our partners and the Project Team (see Appendix B - Partners). Model outputs were further refined based on feedback provided by local knowledge holders beyond the planning team. Accessibility models were inspired by fish-passage prioritization approaches developed by British Columbia’s Fish Passage Technical Working Group.

We recognize the incredible fish passage and connectivity work that has occurred in the Eagle River, Bessette Creek, and Bonaparte River watersheds to date, and we are excited to continue partnering with local groups and organizations to build up existing initiatives and provide a road map to push connectivity restoration forward over the next 10 years and beyond.

Secwépemc Fisheries Commission (SFC) and the Canadian Wildlife Federation (CWF) recognize that the lands and waters that form the basis of this plan are the traditional unceded territory of the Splatsin te Secwépemc and St’uxwtéws. We are grateful for the opportunity to learn from the stewards of this land and work together to benefit Pacific Salmon and steelhead.
